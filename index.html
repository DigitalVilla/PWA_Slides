About Mobile development

Companies that understand the tight relationship between internet and mobile have maximized their customer acquisition by either making their website experience closer to that of a native app or creating an entire mobile version of their current website.

Why Should you learn it ?

if you are a developer, entrepreneur or a startup who has not yet embraced the “mobile first” approach, you are becoming rapidly an endangered species.

Native App

Native Compiled

Development

If you have fifty grand just for development go Native, with moderate money or time to learn a new technology you cannot go wrong with React Native. If you have money but you’d rather pocket it because your business does not require fancy hardware access get a PWA. Only if you are very limited in time and money pick Cordova or a similar hybrid tool, but there is not a real demand for it.

Native App

  • Required Technologies: Swift for iOS and Kotlin/Java for Android.
  • Pros: Best performance and best support for the mobile devices' hardware.
  • Cons: Most expensive, longest development and slowest to learn.
  • Companies using it: Everyone with an app that is not in the other categories.

Native Compiled

  • Required Technologies: React Native or flutter.
  • Pros: Second-best performer, fast development (cheaper than native) and runs in multiple platforms with the same code.
  • Cons:Steep learning curve and not purely native.
  • Companies using it: Facebook, Walmart, Instagram and SoundCloud.

PWA App

  • Required Technologies: JSON and JavaScript.
  • Pros:Most flexible, highly customizable, great user experience, feels and acts like a native app.
  • Cons:Limited access to hardware such as fingerprint scanning, NFC and Geo-Fencing.
  • Companies using it: Forbes, Twitter, Lancome, Airbnb, Financial Post and Alibaba.

Hybrid Applications

  • Required Technologies: Cordova / Ionic / Xamarin.
  • Pros: The easiest to use, cheapest to develop and the fastest to implement.
  • Cons: Lowest performance, not very stable and bad for real world production.
  • Companies using it: No one polular or consumer centered.
